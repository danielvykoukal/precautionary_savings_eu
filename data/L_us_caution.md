# Why Europeans are more cautious savers -- equity involvement, US vs EA
########################################################################

Risky (equity & funds, F5) share of household financial assets (2025):
  United States : 57%
  Euro area     : 37%
  -> US households hold far more equity (about 1.5x the euro-area share); the gap is large and persistent. Non-risky deposits (F2) are the mirror image: euro-area households hold far more in cash & deposits.

Why (history): the US built a funded, equity-heavy private-pension system (401(k)/IRA) and an equity-investing culture; continental Europe leaned on pay-as-you-go STATE pensions and bank deposits. So European households carry less market risk and look more 'cautious' -- a structural, institutional difference, not just preferences.
